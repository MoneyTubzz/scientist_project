Dr. Michio Kaku, PhD

Born: January 24, 1947 In San Jose, USA
Kaku is a renowned theoretical physicist, futurist, and professor at the City College of New York, where he is credited as the co-founder of string field theory. Known for making complex scientific ideas accessible and engaging, he has authored several bestselling books, including Physics of the Impossible and The Future of the Mind. Kaku also hosts the radio program "Science Fantastic" and frequently appears on television, podcasts, and documentaries, where he discusses topics such as the future of technology, space exploration, the nature of the universe, quantum computing, and so much more!

Hyperspace and a Theory of Everything explores the idea of higher dimensions and their role in unifying the forces of nature. Using a childhood memory of a carp in a pond as a metaphor, the article connects this limited worldview to our own potential ignorance of higher dimensions. It discusses the quest for a "Theory of Everything" to unify gravity, electromagnetism, and nuclear forces, and introduces superstring theory, which suggests that tiny vibrating strings in a 10-dimensional space could explain all forces. The article ends with an optimistic call for future generations to complete this groundbreaking theory.
